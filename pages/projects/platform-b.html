OCaml Platform

The OCaml Platform is a collection of libraries, tools and documentation that is released as a consistent, tested bundle. It includes the standard OCaml compiler released by INRIA and community contributions. The OCaml Platform will be considered a success if it comes out regularly, is of high quality, and becomes the standard way for developers to get OCaml and its packages. This is not a solo effort, as other collaborators such as OCamlPro are developing packaging and build systems that form a key part of the overall platform releases.

Building an OCaml Platform community site and infrastructure (continuous build, hosted packages and cross-platform testing) is one of the first tasks that OCamlLabs will tackle. This should be an interesting exercise in eating our own dogfood, as most of the infrastructure is being built in OCaml itself, with many of the libraries written here in Cambridge. There is a heavy emphasis on automation, such as using Xen to spin up different operating systems as VMs to perform build and unit tests on all support packages. The more automation there is, the more we can focus on expanding and evolving the Platform without regressions.

You can get involved with the OCaml Platform efforts immediately by joining the mailing list. If you are an industrial user of OCaml and want to make your needs known, then please do join and speak up! We will be regularly updating the main OCaml list with the results of our work, and updating the news page. And of course, remember that we are hiring at several levels!
